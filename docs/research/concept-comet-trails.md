## Concept: SYZYGY — The Twin-Comet Duet

### Fantasy
The room goes dark and you are somewhere very high and very quiet — a night-space with a faint aurora lattice hanging in it like frozen weather. A golden comet wakes, sings, and carves a glowing road through the dark: rising when the voice rises, blooming wide on open vowels, whipping bright on consonants, leaving a river of embers that shows you exactly where a voice *goes* when it does that thing you love. Then it waits at the start of its road, breathing. You inhale, and a second comet — yours, blue-white, made of the same star-stuff — ignites at your lips' first sound. You are not reading a score; you are *flying*. When you find the pitch, you feel the pull: filaments of light reach between the two bodies, your trail starts drinking gold, theirs drinks blue. When you truly land inside the voice, the comets fall into each other's orbit, braid their tails, and fuse into one iridescent sun — and for a second the space rings like a struck bell of light. Missing never hurts; missing just means you painted a different, farther river in the sky, and you can see — bodily, instantly — which way home is.

### The space
The world is a still, dark volume — "the Reach" — with a barely-visible fixed lattice (thin aurora threads at constant world positions) and a dark-glass floor that catches faint reflections. The geometry is a frozen acoustic coordinate frame:

- **Y (up/down) = pitch**, in absolute log-f0. 1 semitone = 1 fixed world unit, always. Higher is literally higher — the one spatial metaphor every human already has for pitch.
- **X (left/right) = vowel frontness (F2)**: /i/ left, /u/ right (mirrorable per locale, but frozen per install).
- **Z (depth) = vowel openness (F1)**: closed vowels near camera, open vowels deeper. Z range is deliberately shallow (≈1/3 of X) and reinforced by floor reflections and size attenuation, so depth reads without stereo vision.
- **Time is not an axis** — time is *motion*. The past is the trail; the future is the ghost road.

**Stability guarantee:** the frame is calibrated **once per target clip, from the target only** — Y centered on the target's median f0, X/Z centered on the target's vowel centroid using fixed formant-to-unit scaling (no per-attempt normalization, no refitting, ever). User audio can never move the axes; it can only move the user's comet. The lattice, camera rig, and ghost road are therefore pixel-identical across attempts 1 through 100 — the space becomes a *place* you learn, like a level in Journey. User pitch outside the calibrated window soft-compresses logarithmically near the ceiling/floor, where the lattice glows faintly to say "edge of the singable world," never clipping or rescaling.

Non-positional acoustic qualities (brightness, tilt, timbre, loudness) live **on the bodies**, not in the coordinates — so the space stays low-dimensional and pre-attentive while the comets stay expressive.

### The entities
Both comets are built from the identical particle substance — the same shader, same physics, same "gas" — differing only in **polarity and temperature-tint**, which is what makes merging possible and gorgeous.

- **Anatomy (both):** a dense *nucleus* (~2k particles in a tight gravitational knot), a *corona* (soft volumetric glow), and a *trail* (up to ~60k particles emitted per comet, aging over ~2.5s of world-time, plus a persistent thin "ember ridge" ribbon marking the exact path centerline).
- **The Target — "the Caller":** warm gold/amber. Its particles are **accretive**: they spiral *inward*, giving it a calm, gathered, inviting character. It is always the one with a road.
- **The User — "the Chaser":** cool cyan/ice-white. Its particles are **emissive**: they stream *outward and back*, giving it eagerness and effort. It is always the one in motion toward something.
- Gold-vs-cyan survives all common color-vision deficiencies, and polarity (inward vs outward particle flow) is a second, colorblind-proof identity channel readable in peripheral vision.
- **Same substance:** because both are one particle system with a per-particle `owner` scalar, particles can be *exchanged* — when close, trail particles near the midpoint smoothly lerp owner-tint, so the trails visibly drink each other's color. At full merge the owner scalar averages and the fused body renders iridescent (gold and cyan interference film), not "one comet eating the other." Merging is a chemical wedding, not a victory of one over the other.

### Mapping table

| Acoustic signal | Visual channel | Perceptual reading |
|---|---|---|
| Pitch (log-f0) | Comet's vertical (Y) position; 1 semitone = fixed unit | "The voice is high/low; it rose a third" — read as altitude |
| Voicing confidence | Nucleus cohesion: voiced = tight bright knot; unvoiced = nucleus dissolves into a drifting breath-mist (still colored, still present) | "That part is breath, not tone" — whispers look like fog, notes look like stars |
| Loudness / energy | Comet mass: nucleus radius, corona intensity, trail emission rate (springy, ~80ms attack / ~250ms release) | "Louder = bigger and hotter" — swells visibly *swell* |
| Spectral centroid (brightness) | Nucleus **color temperature** on a blackbody ramp under the identity tint: dark = deep ember, bright = blue-white sear; plus corona sharpness | "A brighter, more forward sound literally glows hotter and sharper" |
| Spectral tilt | Trail plume texture: steep tilt (dark/warm voice) = soft, rounded, feathered plume; flat tilt (pressed/bright) = spiky, filamented, crystalline plume | "Smoothness vs edge of the voice" read as fur vs quills |
| F1 (openness) | Z depth position (+ floor reflection offset) | "Open your mouth = the comet dives deeper into the space" |
| F2 (frontness) | X lateral position | "ee is over there, oo is over here" — vowels are *places* |
| Onset / rhythm events | A shockwave ring pulse from the nucleus + a bright **bead knotted into the trail** at that point | Rhythm becomes bead-spacing along the trail; two trails' beads either line up like stitching or visibly syncopate |
| Timbre embedding drift (2–4 Hz) | Slow iridescent **hue-film** over the particle gas (thin-film interference tint drifting within the identity color); low-pass eased | "The *character* of the voice" — a nasal target shimmers differently than a breathy one; you match it like matching a mood |
| DTW alignment position | The **pace bead**: a bright droplet gliding along the target's ghost road marking "where you should be now"; the user's leash filament anchors to it | "That's my spot on the road" |
| Timing offset (ms) | Arc-length gap between user comet and pace bead **along the road**, shown by the leash filament stretching forward (dragging: taut, ember-red-shifted shimmer at rear) or backward (rushing: slack loop, blue shimmer at front) | "I'm behind/ahead" felt as being on a leash — the tug direction is the correction |
| Pitch error (semitones) | Pure **vertical gap** between comets (axis-aligned by construction), plus the connecting filament bending vertically | "I'm *under* it" — the fix is literally "go up" |
| Vowel-space distance | Horizontal/depth gap; when it's the dominant error, the lattice threads local to the two comets glimmer on the X/Z plane | "Slide left / open up" as a sideways pull |
| Brightness gap | Color-temperature mismatch between the two nuclei — one ember, one searing — plus filaments flicker instead of flowing | "Their star burns hotter than mine" — fix by brightening the sound |
| **Composite closeness** (all dims) | Resonance stack, in order of onset: (1) 1–5 spring filaments connect the comets (count/brightness = closeness), (2) particle exchange between trails, (3) shared harmonic corona rings, (4) subtle gravitational-lensing warp of the lattice between them, (5) orbit capture → merge | Pre-attentive in <200ms: *two lights near and threaded* vs *two lights far and separate* — readable before any conscious decoding |

### Choreography of a 10-second attempt
- **t = −4s, Idle.** The Reach breathes: lattice threads pulse at ~0.1 Hz, the gold Caller floats at its road's origin, slowly accreting, its full **ghost road** pre-rendered as a faint dust-river through the space (the entire attempt's shape visible at a glance — pitch contour, vowel wander, onset beads all legible as geography). Your cyan comet idles near the camera, dim, mist-bodied, waiting for voice. Live mic input already animates it faintly — clearing your throat makes it shiver. The instrument is alive before the game begins.
- **t = −4→0s, The Caller performs.** The target clip plays; the gold comet flies its road *live* — rising, diving into open vowels, flaring beads on every consonant, its temperature and plume texture dancing with brightness and tilt. This is the choreography lesson: you watch a voice as a flight path. The road brightens behind it like a runway being lit. It returns to origin; three soft lattice pulses count you in (light only, works muted).
- **t = 0s, Ignition.** Your first voiced frame snaps the cyan comet from mist to knot with a critically-damped spring pop (120ms) — instant "it heard me" proprioception. The pace bead starts gliding down the ghost road.
- **t = 0–3s, The chase.** Your comet doesn't teleport to analysis frames — it's flown by stiff springs (~12 Hz natural frequency, near-critical damping) toward the acoustic state, so motion feels muscular, not twitchy. You're a quarter-tone flat: you *see* yourself riding just under the ember ridge, filament bent upward like a kite string. You ease up; the filament straightens, thickens, and begins to *flow* — moving light along it, from you to the bead. Eye-haptics: every correct correction is rewarded by easing (spring settle), never by a popup.
- **t = 3–4.5s, Mismatch moment.** The target dives to an open /a/; you stay closed. The comets shear apart in X/Z; filaments thin to one strand and stretch taut — elastic, luminous *tension*, like taffy of light. The lensing between bodies releases; the lattice flattens. Nothing flashes, nothing turns red. The leash also tugs backward: you hesitated, you're 180ms late — you feel it as slack snapping out of the line.
- **t = 4.5–7s, Convergence.** You open the vowel and catch up. Filaments multiply 1→3→5, particle exchange begins — your trail veining with gold, the road's dust drifting into your plume. Beads from your onsets start landing *on top of* the road's beads: stitching. The lattice between the comets begins to lens, space itself bowing toward the meeting.
- **t = 7–8.5s, Lock-on & coalescence.** All per-dimension errors under threshold for 700ms → **orbit capture**: your comet stops chasing and falls into a tight mutual binary with the Caller (they co-rotate around the pace bead, ~1.2 rev/s, tails braiding into a double helix). Hold it 800ms more → the nuclei fuse: one iridescent sun, gold-cyan interference film, a single soft lens-bloom shockwave rolls through the lattice, and every particle in both trails flares once, in sequence, back along the paths — the whole attempt lights up like a fuse burning in reverse. This is the ring-of-the-bell moment; it is *earned*, never given.
- **t = 8.5–10s, Afterglow.** The fused sun exhales, splits gently back into two courteous bodies. Both full trails **crystallize** — freeze into glassy, faintly glowing paths hanging in the space, yours and theirs, twined or apart.
- **Replay ghost.** One tap: two ember-lights fly both crystallized trails simultaneously in synced time (DTW-aligned or raw-time, toggleable), filaments re-forming live so you can *re-watch the relationship*. Scrubbing drags both lights along their trails. Past attempts can stack as progressively dimmer crystal trails — your practice history becomes visible geology: rivers converging on the gold road across attempts.

### Failure aesthetics
Bad matching is rendered as **distance and weather, never damage**. A wildly off attempt produces two beautiful comets in a wide, slow gravitational dance — filaments become sparse aurora curtains between them rather than threads; the user's trail is still a complete, luminous, *interesting* river, just a different one. Design laws: (1) **No red, no shake, no shrink** — the user's comet never loses mass, dims, or gets visually "sick" for being wrong; loudness alone governs its vitality. (2) **Errors point home** — every mismatch channel is a *direction* (up, left, deeper, hotter, sooner), so failure reads as "the pull is that way," a compass rather than a verdict. (3) **The floor of beauty is high** — even zero resonance effects leaves two living comets and crystallized twin trails worth screenshotting; the afterglow always frames both trails together as a duet portrait ("look how far you traveled"), and across attempts the stacked crystal history shows convergence trends, which reframes failure as approach. (4) Prolonged silence or giving up mid-attempt just lets the comet exhale back to mist while the Caller finishes its road alone, gently — an invitation, not a game-over.

### Implementation sketch
**Particle core (GPGPU):** One unified simulation for both comets: ping-pong RGBA16F FBOs (position/velocity/age/owner-and-state packed across two textures) at 512×512 = 262k budget, updated in a fragment shader (WebGL2). Per-particle behavior blends three fields: attraction to its comet's spring-driven head, curl-noise turbulence (scaled by spectral-tilt for feathered-vs-spiky plumes), and inward-vs-outward radial flow keyed by `owner` polarity. Particle exchange is one lerp of the owner scalar for particles inside the midpoint capture radius. Rendered as instanced point sprites with soft additive blending, temperature/tint from a small LUT. Cost: 2 sim passes + 1 draw ≈ 2–3 ms on mid-tier GPUs; particle count LOD (256×256 tier) for weak devices.

**Comet heads & springs (CPU, trivial):** Analysis frames arrive at 50 Hz; each head is driven by per-axis critically-damped springs (stiff on Y for pitch snappiness, softer on X/Z) plus a mass/temperature spring set. All resonance logic (filament count, exchange radius, orbit-capture state machine with 700ms hysteresis) is a few hundred CPU ops per frame — negligible; smoothing lives here so the GPU never sees jitter. DTW alignment is slew-limited and monotonicity-clamped before it drives the pace bead.

**Ghost road & crystallized trails:** The target's path is pre-baked offline into a 1D texture (position, temperature, tilt, bead flags per 20ms) → rendered as (a) a camera-facing ribbon with a dust-scatter shader for the faint road, (b) a brighter "ember ridge" polyline, (c) instanced bead sprites at onsets. Crystallized replay trails reuse the same pipeline fed from the recorded attempt buffer; replay is just animating a parameter `t` along two baked textures. Cost: <1 ms; fully static geometry, zero per-frame CPU.

**Filaments, lensing, bloom (post/FX):** Filaments are 1–5 instanced quadratic-Bézier ribbons (control point at the sag/tension midpoint; flow via scrolling emissive noise; ~200 verts each — trivial). Gravitational lensing is a screen-space UV-warp pass: two radial distortion kernels at the comets' projected positions whose strength = closeness, applied before bloom (one full-screen pass). Bloom is a standard 5-level mip down/up chain at half-res with soft-knee threshold (~1.5 ms). Lattice and floor reflection are static meshes with a cheap planar-reflection fake (render comets' billboards flipped, 20% opacity). Total frame budget ≈ 6–8 ms — comfortable 60fps headroom.

### Learn Mode degradation
Learn Mode is not a different screen — it is **the same world, unfolded**. The camera rig owns four saved orthographic stations, and transitioning is a continuous camera+space morph (800ms, eased), so meaning is preserved by literal continuity: (1) **Pitch panel** = fly to the side view while the ghost road *unrolls* — the road's arc-length parameter maps to a straight time axis, and suddenly you're looking at a pitch-over-time lane chart, except the "lines" are the same crystallized trails, same gold/cyan, with semitone lattice threads becoming gridlines and the filaments becoming per-moment error whiskers. (2) **Vowel panel** = fly to top-down: the X/Z plane becomes the classic F1/F2 vowel chart; IPA anchor glyphs fade in at the lattice intersections; both comets' vowel wanderings are their trails seen from above. (3) **Timing panel** = the road straightens and the onset beads of both voices sit on parallel rails — bead-to-bead offsets get millisecond labels. (4) **Timbre/brightness panel** = both nuclei enlarge side-by-side; temperature ramp and iridescent hue-film get labeled scales (centroid Hz, tilt dB/oct, embedding-similarity dial). Every panel is a projection of geometry the user already navigated bodily, so numbers arrive as *annotations on a place they know*, not a new product. Tapping any moment in any panel warps the 3D view back to that instant of the replay.

### Risks & mitigations
1. **Depth (Z = F1) is hard to read on a 2D screen** → keep Z range shallow (⅓ of X), reinforce with three redundant cues (size attenuation, dark-glass floor reflections, depth fog), bias the default camera 15° above horizon so Z partially projects to screen-Y offset from the reflection; validate in playtests and, if still weak, collapse to a 2.5D mapping (F1 modulates comet vertical *squash* + floor-shadow offset instead of true depth) without touching any other system.
2. **DTW jitter makes the pace bead / leash twitch and destroys trust** → slew-limit alignment velocity (max ±1.5× real-time), enforce monotonic progress with hysteresis, drive the leash from a 150ms low-passed offset, and freeze the bead (gentle pulsing) during unvoiced/silent user stretches rather than guessing; the leash communicates *tendency* (rushing/dragging), not raw milliseconds — milliseconds live in Learn Mode.
3. **Resonance-effect stack overwhelms the core read (visual noise arms race)** → strict luminance budget: composite closeness owns bloom/lensing exclusively; per-dimension cues are capped at low intensity and only the *dominant* error dimension gets its secondary cue at any moment (softmax gating); ship a "quiet mode" that drops to comets + filaments + road only — the 200ms distance read must survive with everything else turned off, and is A/B tested that way.
