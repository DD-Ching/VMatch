## Concept: SELVEDGE

### Fantasy
You stand before a loom strung in darkness. The voice you want to become is already on it — a ribbon of ember-gold silk, woven and waiting, rising and falling across the warp like a phrase held in amber. When it sings, light runs down its length and you watch it *move*: swelling wide where the voice opens, climbing the warp where the pitch lifts, knotting bright where syllables strike. Then it's your turn. Your breath becomes a second ribbon — silver, cool, alive — spinning out of you in real time beside the gold. When you're close, the two silks lean toward each other, catch the same light, and begin to braid; a shiver of resonance runs down both threads at once and you feel it in your chest like a chord locking. When you drift, your ribbon doesn't die — it billows away like silk in wind, still tethered by gossamer threads that always point home. At the end, the fabric of your attempt hangs on the loom: not a score, a *cloth* — something you wove together, that you can run your eyes back along and read.

### The space
The world is a loom seen side-on in a warm dark void. Its geometry is fixed and literal:

- **X (horizontal) = time.** A luminous vertical line — the **beater** — is "now." Fabric already sung accumulates to the left of it (the woven past); the target's remaining phrase waits to the right as a dim pre-woven pattern (the visible future). The world scrolls right-to-left past the beater at the target's tempo; the camera never moves. This is the single most important stability decision: a fixed now-line means the eye always knows where to look.
- **Y (vertical) = log-pitch.** Behind everything hangs the **warp**: faint horizontal threads at semitone spacing, fitted **once** from the target clip's pitch range (±4 semitones of headroom) and cached with the exercise. It never refits between attempts — the same note is always the same height, forever, so muscle memory and eye memory compound.
- **Z (shallow depth, ±10% of frame) = F2 (vowel frontness)** — a gentle near/far lean, deliberately secondary; vowel is read mainly through ribbon *twist* and specular light (below), not depth, so the primary error channels (pitch gap, timing shear) stay in the flat screen plane where humans are pre-attentively fastest.

Distance between the two ribbon centerlines at the beater **is** the match. Vertical gap = pitch error, longitudinal slippage of features = timing error. Nothing to decode: closer is closer. Readable in a saccade, well under 200ms.

### The entities
Both voices are **the same silk** — a ribbon whose cross-section is a flat band of ~9 virtual threads, shaded with anisotropic sheen and a woven micro-texture. Same substance, so they can braid and merge into one cloth. Identity is carried by three constant, non-semantic channels:

- **Target ribbon:** ember-gold base hue, S-twist thread direction in its micro-weave, slightly matte selvedge (edge thread).
- **User ribbon:** moon-silver base hue, Z-twist weave, brighter selvedge.
- The target is **pre-woven**: its full phrase exists on the loom from the start, resting in a dim "ember" state; playback and progress *illuminate* it rather than create it. The user ribbon is **extruded live** from a small comet of light (the **shuttle**) sitting at the beater — the user's mouth, in the world.

Unvoiced sound (fricatives, breath, whispers) has no f0, so the ribbon honestly changes state: it unbinds into a **mist of particles** drifting along the same path — breath rendered as breath. When voicing returns, the mist snaps back into silk with a soft spring overshoot. Merging: when all closeness signals lock, the hues don't average into grey — they resolve into shared **white-gold iridescence** (thin-film shimmer) while each ribbon keeps its selvedge color, so identity survives inside the merge.

### Mapping table

| Acoustic signal | Visual channel | Perceptual reading |
|---|---|---|
| log-f0 | Ribbon centerline height on the warp (fixed semitone lattice) | "The voice is here in pitch" — position, the strongest pre-attentive channel |
| Voicing confidence | Silk solidity: 1.0 = woven band → 0 = particle mist along the path | "Tone vs breath" — texture state, honest to the signal |
| Loudness / energy | Ribbon width (log-mapped, spring-smoothed) | "How much voice" — swelling and thinning like breath itself |
| Spectral centroid (brightness) | Silk color temperature + emissive luminance (dark amber → white-hot) | "Bright vs dark tone" — literally brighter silk |
| Spectral tilt | Sheen character: steep tilt = soft satin diffuse; flat tilt = hard metallic anisotropic glint | "Warm/covered vs edgy/pressed" — how the silk catches light |
| F1 (vowel height) | Cross-section **roll** of the band (face-up ↔ face-forward, ~±60°) | "Open vs closed mouth" — the ribbon's facing |
| F2 (vowel frontness) | Shallow Z lean + weft hue tint (front = cyan-shift, back = magenta-shift of the micro-threads) | "Where the vowel lives" |
| Onset / rhythm events | **Pick knots**: a bright transverse thread beaten into the ribbon at each onset, with a 120ms light pulse | "The beats of the phrase" — countable, rhythmic punctuation |
| Timbre embedding (2–4 Hz) | Slow-morphing woven micro-pattern (procedural weave motif) + iridescence phase | "The grain of this voice" — character, not moment |
| DTW alignment (position on target timeline) | The user's shuttle rides the target ribbon's illuminated progress point; correspondence rendered as **weft threads** stitched between matched points of the two ribbons every ~100ms | "I am *here* in their phrase" |
| Pitch error (semitones) | Vertical gap between centerlines at the beater; warp threads between them glow faintly, countable as rungs | "Two semitones flat" read as *two rungs below* — geometric, not judgmental |
| Timing offset (ms) | **Shear** of the weft threads: aligned = short perpendicular stitches; early/late = stitches slant forward/back; user's pick knots visibly lead/lag target's | "I'm rushing" felt as forward-leaning threads |
| Vowel-space distance | Twist mismatch: matched vowels mean both bands catch the key light together and flash one shared specular **glint**; mismatched faces glint out of sync | "Same mouth shape" as synchronized flash |
| Brightness gap | Color-temperature difference between ribbons (one warm, one cool until they meet) | "Match their tone color" as literal color convergence |
| Composite closeness (all dims) | **Braid drive**: below threshold, ribbons run parallel; above it, they begin helixing around a shared axis, braid period tightening with sustained closeness; a resonance wave shimmers down both | "We are becoming one thread" |

### Choreography of a 10-second attempt
- **0.0–1.5s — Idle / the pattern on the loom.** Dark. Warp threads breathe at ~0.1 Hz (2% opacity oscillation). The target ribbon lies fully pre-woven in ember state across the frame — the user can already *read* the whole phrase's shape: its climbs, its swells, its knots. The silver shuttle idles at the beater, pulsing gently with the user's ambient mic level (proof of life: "it hears me").
- **1.5–4.0s — Target performs.** A pulse of light (the golden shuttle) enters from the right and runs the target ribbon at performance speed. Each section *wakes* as the light passes: width swells with loudness, the band rolls through its vowel twists, pick knots strike with a soft bloom flash exactly on onsets. The eye rehearses the choreography before the body tries it. The wake decays over ~2s back toward ember (exponential ease-out), leaving the path known.
- **4.0s — Handoff.** The beater line brightens; a 3-2-1 of three warp threads glowing in sequence (no numerals). The world begins scrolling. The user's shuttle ignites.
- **4.0–7.5s — The attempt.** The user sings. Silver silk extrudes from the shuttle in real time, laid against the gold. Every 20ms frame appends to the ribbon through a critically-damped spring (~80ms settle) — motion feels muscular, not jittery. Weft threads stitch across every ~100ms as DTW binds the timelines. **Match moment (say 5.2s):** pitch gap closes under 0.5 semitone with vowels aligned — both bands catch the key light and fire one shared glint; the braid drive engages with a slow ease-in (400ms) and the ribbons begin a lazy helix; a shimmer wave runs *backward* down both ribbons into the already-woven past, retroactively blessing it. **Mismatch moment (say 6.1s):** user goes sharp and late — silver lifts two warp-rungs above gold, weft threads stretch and slant forward, going gossamer-taut; the braid unwinds over 600ms (never snaps); the silver ribbon's edges fray into a few dozen fireflies that drift *down toward* the gold — the error literally points at the correction.
- **7.5–8.5s — Lock-on climax.** Sustained closeness on all dimensions for >700ms: braid period tightens from 1.2s to 0.4s, the helix pulls the ribbons into contact, hues resolve to white-gold iridescence, and a standing wave — driven by the shared f0's vibrato — undulates along the merged braid. Bloom widens ~30% for 500ms then relaxes. This is the "chord in the chest" moment; it is earned, never faked.
- **8.5–10s — Afterglow / the cloth.** Phrase ends. Scrolling ends with a decelerating ease (cubic, 800ms) — the beater releases like a held breath. The camera pulls back 15% to reveal the whole woven artifact: braided where you matched, parallel where you were close, billowed where you drifted. It hangs there, softly lit. **Replay ghost:** tap it and both shuttles run the fabric together at any speed; scrub by dragging along the cloth itself — the ribbon *is* the timeline. Successive attempts stack as layers in a bolt of cloth; progress is visible as later layers braiding earlier and tighter.

### Failure aesthetics
Failure is **wind, never breakage.** A badly-missed section renders as the silver ribbon billowing wide of the gold — turbulent, undulating, genuinely beautiful, like silk loose in a draft — but three rules keep it legible and kind: (1) **soft tether**: separation is soft-clamped to ~9 semitones of visual travel; the weft threads never detach, only stretch to gossamer, so the relationship is never lost and the screen never empties; (2) **errors point home**: fraying particles always drift toward the target ribbon, and slanted weft threads always lean in the direction of the correction — every failure state is also an instruction; (3) **no punitive channel exists**: there is no red, no shake, no darkness-as-shame; brightness maps to spectral centroid only, so a wildly wrong attempt still produces a luminous, interesting cloth. Crucially, the woven artifact reframes failure as *material*: a turbulent stretch of fabric reads as "here's where it got windy," a place to revisit, not a grade. Partial credit is structural — even one matched dimension (right rhythm, wrong pitch) visibly aligns the pick knots while the ribbons stay apart, so users always see *what they got right*.

### Implementation sketch
**Ribbon geometry.** Per-voice acoustic state (f0, voicing, loudness, centroid, tilt, F1/F2, timbre phase) is written each 20ms frame into a ring-buffer float data texture (e.g., 2048×2 RGBA32F). Ribbons are static index buffers (~1200 segments × 10 verts across) whose vertex shader reads the ring buffer, builds the centerline (x from buffer index vs. scroll offset, y from log-f0 against the cached warp calibration, z from F2), applies band roll from F1, and width from loudness. No CPU geometry per frame; both ribbons cost one draw call each, <0.5ms.

**Silk shading.** Fragment shader: Kajiya-Kay/GGX anisotropic specular with tangent along the thread direction (S vs Z twist flips the tangent field — the cheap identity cue), a procedural woven normal/AO micro-pattern whose motif parameters lerp at the timbre embedding's 2–4 Hz rate, thin-film iridescence term ramped in by the merge state, and emissive term from spectral centroid feeding the bloom threshold. Half-res UnrealBloom-style pyramid (~1.2ms at 1080p).

**Breath mist & fray particles.** GPGPU: position/velocity in two ping-pong RGBA32F textures, 256×256 = 65k particles typical (headroom to 200k at 512×512, ~1.5ms). Forces: advection along the parent ribbon's stored path, curl noise for turbulence scaled by |closeness error|, and a soft attractor toward the target ribbon's corresponding DTW point (this is what makes errors "point home"). Spawn/kill by writing lifetimes keyed to voicing confidence and fray state.

**Weft threads & braid dynamics.** DTW correspondences (slew-rate-limited to ≤2 frames/frame to kill alignment jumps) emit one instanced quadratic-bezier strip per ~100ms — ~40 live instances, negligible cost; slant comes free from the endpoints. Braiding is *not* cloth sim: the rendered user centerline is `mix(rawUserState, braidParametrization(targetCenterline, θ(t)), w)` where `w` is the smoothed composite closeness and θ advances with tightness — a critically-damped spring on `w` gives the ease-in/unwind. Deterministic, stable, cheap, and it can never lie by more than the blend the closeness signal has earned.

**Frame budget.** Ribbons ~0.7ms, particles ~1.5ms, weft ~0.1ms, bloom ~1.4ms, warp/background ~0.3ms → ~4ms GPU at 1080p; comfortable 60fps on WebGL2 integrated GPUs, with particle count and bloom resolution as the two quality dials.

### Learn Mode degradation
Learn Mode is an **unweaving**, not a different screen. The camera glides to true orthographic side view and the world stops scrolling; the warp lattice gains semitone labels and note names — the ribbon centerlines over the labeled warp *are* the pitch panel, unchanged. Pinch vertically and the ribbons flatten: the band's roll unrolls into a small F1/F2 vowel plane beneath the timeline, with both voices' vowel trajectories drawn as the same gold and silver threads. The pick knots project down onto a rhythm rail (target knots above, user knots below, timing offsets as the same slanted stitches, now with ms labels on tap). The silk's color strip peels off as a brightness/tilt band with a temperature scale. Tap any stretch of fabric to loop it with all panels live. Everything keeps its material, hue, and identity — the panels are the ribbon's own properties laid flat — so Learn Mode feels like leaning closer to the loom, and re-weaving (zooming back out) returns the exact same world.

### Risks & mitigations
1. **Live-input jitter makes the silver ribbon look nervous, poisoning the "living silk" feel.** Mitigate: one-euro filter on f0/F1/F2 upstream, critically-damped spring (≈80ms) on all geometric channels, render-side interpolation between 20ms analysis frames; tune so vibrato (5–7 Hz) survives but octave-flip glitches don't (median-of-3 + voicing gate on f0).
2. **Braided/merged ribbons occlude each other, hiding the target right when the user needs it.** Mitigate: braiding only engages *when matched* (occlusion literally cannot occur during error states); during braid, a 4° camera parallax drift plus the two selvedge colors keep both threads countable; depth-peeled edge glow keeps the occluded ribbon's silhouette readable.
3. **DTW alignment jumps cause the weft threads and shuttle to teleport, breaking spatial trust.** Mitigate: slew-limit the alignment cursor (≤2 analysis frames of travel per frame), render alignment confidence as weft opacity so low-confidence stretches fade rather than flail, and freeze the braid drive (hold last `w`, no penalty) whenever confidence drops below threshold.
