## Concept: SYMPATHY — The Listening Water

### Fantasy
You stand at the edge of a dark, still pool at night — obsidian water lit from below. A voice you want to become lives under the surface: when it sings, deep teal rings bloom upward through the water, tracing a glowing path across the pool like bioluminescence remembering a melody. When you sing, your own light rides *on top* of the same water — warm gold ripples spreading from a second bright point. You are not being graded; you are two disturbances in one body of water, and the water cannot lie. When you drift, the surface chops into crosscurrents and shimmering moiré — beautiful, restless, unresolved. As you close in, you feel it before you can name it: the throb between your two wave-patterns slows... slows... stills. The rings lock concentric. The whole pool crystallizes into one breathing cymatic figure, teal and gold flooding into white, and for a held breath you and the other voice are a single standing wave. The pool remembers: your paths float up as paired filaments of light, twin ghosts you can replay and study, side by side in the same water.

### The space
The world is a single shallow **resonant pool**: a fixed rectangular water/light surface viewed from a fixed, slightly elevated camera (~25° pitch, no camera motion, ever). It is one continuous physical medium — a GPU wave-field — but its *coordinates* are acoustic and permanent:

- **Vertical axis (toward/away from viewer on the surface) = pitch**, in log-f0 semitones. Up-screen = higher pitch. Embodied and universal.
- **Horizontal axis = brightness** (spectral centroid, log-scaled). Right = brighter/more forward; left = darker/rounder.
- **Everything else lives in the *behavior of the waves*, not in position**: vowel is the *shape* of the ripple pattern, loudness is amplitude, timing is ring phase, timbre is the water's color-body.

**Why it stays stable:** the axis scales are computed **once, when the target clip loads** — pitch range = target's f0 range padded ±6 semitones; brightness range = target's centroid range padded ±20%. These scales are frozen for the whole session and across attempts. No auto-refit, no renormalization mid-attempt, no PCA — the pool is the same pool every time, so muscle memory of "where E4 lives on this water" carries across attempts. Faint bathymetric isolines (semitone contours) sleep in the water at 3% opacity, invisible until Learn Mode wakes them.

Distance between the two glowing epicenters is therefore an honest, geometric composite of pitch error (dominant, vertical) and brightness gap (horizontal) — readable pre-attentively as pure position, within one saccade.

### The entities
Both voices are **excitation sources in the same water** — same substance (waves), different *depth and light*:

- **The Target — "the Undervoice."** A deep-teal/indigo epicenter that glows *beneath* the surface. Its waves are rendered as refracted caustics — light bending up through the water. It reads as older, deeper, already-belonging-to-the-pool. While its clip plays, it glides along its path and leaves a **persistent phosphorescent trace** (the "score-path") with bright knots at every onset event — the choreography, written on the water.
- **The User — "the Overvoice."** A warm gold-white epicenter riding *on* the surface. Its waves are specular — light glinting off the top. It reads as newer, lighter, arriving.

Same anatomy for both: a bright core (voicing), a **cymatic glyph** around the core (the vowel pattern — a petal/lobe figure parameterized by F1/F2), and emitted rings. Identity is carried by three redundant channels that survive full overlap: **hue (teal vs gold — CVD-safe pair), depth layer (refracted vs specular), and ring texture** (target rings are smooth and glassy; user rings carry a fine live grain). Because the substrate is identical, merging is not a hack: when the two wave-fields phase-lock, additive teal + gold naturally floods to **white** — the merge color is earned by optics, not awarded by UI.

Under the hood the medium is **two superposed wave-fields, composited in one render** — so both voices genuinely share one water, but each stays tintable, and their interference is computed explicitly (see Implementation).

### Mapping table

| Acoustic signal | Visual channel | Perceptual reading |
|---|---|---|
| Pitch (log-f0) | Epicenter vertical position (fixed semitone scale) **+** ring emission rate (f0/32 → visible 2–15 Hz, preserving interval ratios) | "How high I am on the water"; faster ripples = higher voice |
| Voicing confidence | Core coherence: voiced = tight bright core emitting clean rings; unvoiced = core dissolves into drifting mist (breath = wind on water, not waves) | "Am I *toning* or just breathing" |
| Loudness / energy | Wave amplitude + core radiance + spray-particle emission rate | Effort you can see; silence = the pool goes glassy |
| Spectral centroid (brightness) | Epicenter horizontal position + color temperature bias of that voice's light (cooler↔warmer within its identity hue) | "How forward/bright my tone sits" |
| Spectral tilt | Wavefront edge quality: steep tilt (dark, breathy) = soft gaussian-edged rings; flat tilt (pressed, bright) = crisp hard-edged rings with harmonic filigree | Texture of the sound made touchable |
| F1/F2 vowel position | Cymatic glyph shape: F1 → radial ring count, F2 → angular petal count; the glyph is stamped into every emitted wavefront | Each vowel is a *figure* — /a/ is an open bloom, /i/ a tight many-petaled star |
| Onset / rhythm events | Impulse splash: one bright circular shockwave + a knot ("buoy") deposited on the trace | Beats are strikes on the water; the score-path is beaded with them |
| Timbre embedding (2–4 Hz) | Slow iridescent color-body of the water *around* each epicenter (oil-film hue drift within each identity family) | The "material" of the voice; two timbres approaching = the two color-bodies bleeding toward each other |
| DTW alignment position | A traveling "now-glow" on the target's score-path showing where the user currently is on the target timeline | "You are *here* in the phrase" without any playhead bar |
| **Pitch error (semitones)** | (a) Vertical gap between epicenters; (b) a light-tether between them that bows *toward the correction* (bows up = sing higher); (c) **beat-throb**: the combined field pulses at the mapped difference frequency — throbbing slows as error shrinks and goes utterly still at unison | The strongest cue: *stillness = truth*. Physically honest beating, learned in one attempt |
| **Timing offset (ms)** | Ring phase lag: user rings arrive early/late where the wavefronts cross; in-time = concentric phase-locked rings, off-time = visible crest slippage | "My waves keep arriving before hers" — timing felt as physics |
| **Vowel-space distance** | Interference clarity between the two glyph patterns: matched vowels superpose into one crisp figure; mismatched vowels produce moiré shimmer between the epicenters | Moiré = "wrong mouth shape," resolving moiré = the figure snapping into focus |
| **Brightness gap** | Horizontal gap + temperature clash of the two light bodies (visibly "different kinds of light") | "Same note, different color" is instantly visible even at zero pitch error |

### Choreography of a 10-second attempt
- **0.0–1.5s — Idle.** Near-black water breathing at ±1px swell. The user's mic is already alive at whisper-gain: their breath stirs faint gold mist — *the pool is listening to me before I've done anything.* The target epicenter sleeps as a dim teal ember at its starting pitch.
- **1.5–4.5s — The target performs.** The Undervoice wakes: teal caustic rings bloom, the epicenter glides its path (pitch/brightness choreography), its vowel glyph morphing /a/→/i/ as it goes, onset buoys dropping at each attack. The path burns into the water as phosphorescent trace. The user watches the dance they're about to join. Ends with the epicenter returning to start; the trace stays.
- **4.5–5.0s — Invitation.** Three soft concentric count-in rings pulse from the target's start point (spring-eased, expo-out). The user's gold epicenter fades in at wherever their (silent) formant/breath state sits — already embodied, already theirs.
- **5.0–8.0s — The attempt.** User sings. Gold rings pour out; their epicenter chases the teal trace while the Undervoice re-performs live at 40% amplitude (duet, not karaoke). Continuous coupled feedback: pitch off by 2 st → clear vertical gap, tether bowed upward, water between them choppy with crosscurrents; user closes to 0.5 st → gap nearly shut, **beat-throb at ~3 Hz, decelerating** as they bend the note in — the deceleration is felt like a heartbeat calming; vowel wrong on "ee" → moiré shimmer flares between the glyphs, then the figures click into one star as the mouth closes; a late onset → the splash lands visibly *behind* the buoy, its shockwave lapping the target's already-spreading ring.
- **Lock-on (any sustained match >300ms, all four closeness signals under threshold).** A **sympathetic pull** engages: mutual spring attraction scaled by closeness (capped at 0.3 st so it only bridges sub-audible error — the snap never lies). Epicenters glide together on a critically-damped spring (~150ms settle), rings go concentric and phase-locked, a **standing-wave figure crystallizes** across the whole pool — the shared cymatic glyph, huge and breathing — and teal+gold additive-bloom to white. The throb is *gone*: total stillness inside the pattern. This is the payoff no split-entity concept can match: not "two things touching" but *one pattern where two used to be.*
- **8.0–8.6s — Release.** Voices end. The standing wave doesn't cut — it **rings out** like a struck bell, amplitude decaying over 600ms, white relaxing back into braided teal-gold.
- **8.6–10s — Afterglow & ghost.** Both trajectories float up off the surface as two luminous filaments hovering a few pixels above the water — the target's trace and the user's actual path, twined where they matched, splayed where they didn't. A single tap replays both: epicenters re-run the attempt at low amplitude, waves and all, so the user can *watch* the moment the throb stilled. Every match segment on the user's filament is annealed white; drift segments stay gold — a self-annotating record, no numbers.

### Failure aesthetics
Bad matching is rendered as **weather, never wounds**. A wide miss doesn't shatter or redden anything — the pool simply holds *two separate calm ponds of light*, each intrinsically beautiful, with quiet water between them: the aesthetic of *distance*, not error. Moderate mismatch is crosscurrent chop and moiré — visually alive, like rain on a lake; users describe it as "unresolved," which is exactly the felt meaning. Three rules keep it kind: (1) **Energy is honored** — loud committed singing always makes gorgeous waves regardless of accuracy; the pool never goes dead on effort, only on silence. (2) **Every failure cue contains its own correction** — the tether bows toward the fix, the throb slows as you approach, moiré resolves continuously; there is no cue that says "wrong" without simultaneously saying "this way." (3) **No red, no depletion, no shrinkage** — the user's entity never dims or shrinks from error; identity light is inviolable. Missed attempts still end with the filament afterglow: even a failed take leaves a beautiful object to study, so retrying feels like sculpting, not re-testing.

### Implementation sketch
**Wave field (core):** Two independent 2D wave-equation simulations (one per voice) on ping-pong half-float FBOs at 512×512, classic two-buffer update `h' = damping·(2h − h_prev + c²∇²h)` in a fragment shader, excited each frame by stamping the analytic cymatic glyph (sum of `cos(kθ)` angular harmonics × radial falloff, k and ring count from F2/F1) at the epicenter UV with amplitude = loudness and stamp cadence = f0/32. Two 512² sims + composite ≈ 0.4ms total on integrated GPUs. Keeping the fields separate is the key trick: identity tinting, per-field readability, and an explicit per-pixel coherence term (`h₁·h₂` normalized) computed at composite time to drive the white-bloom and moiré shading — interference we *style*, powered by superposition that's *real*.

**Surface rendering:** One 512×512 grid mesh displaced by the summed heightfield in the vertex shader; normals from heightfield gradient (dFdx-free, sampled neighbors). Fragment shader: two-layer lighting — refracted under-glow (target field, teal, fake caustic via gradient-warped lookup) + specular sheen (user field, gold) + coherence-driven white bloom term + 3%-opacity isoline layer. ~1ms. Camera and plane never move: all "motion" is in the medium.

**Particles (spray/plankton):** ~150k GPU particles in a texture-based state buffer (position/velocity in FBOs), advected by heightfield gradient + curl noise, spawned at epicenters proportional to loudness, drawn as additive 2px sprites. Update + draw ≈ 1.5ms. They exist purely to make amplitude and lock-on feel *material* (spray converging into the standing-wave nodes at merge).

**Traces & replay ghost:** Score-path and attempt-path stored as CPU polylines in fixed voice-space coordinates (cheap: 50Hz × 10s), rendered as instanced ribbon quads with a phosphor shader; live trail persistence via a fade-accumulate FBO (multiply 0.98/frame). Replay = re-run stored per-frame signal buffers through the same excitation stamping at 0.4× amplitude — zero new code paths, guaranteed visual consistency between live and ghost.

**Springs & feel:** All epicenter motion via critically-damped springs on 1-pole-smoothed signals (pitch spring ~120ms settle to preserve the 200ms readability budget; brightness smoothed harder, ~250ms, since centroid is jittery). Sympathetic pull, tether bow, count-in pulses, lock-on snap: all CPU-side scalar springs, negligible cost. Total frame budget ≈ 4.5ms + ~1ms bloom (threshold + mip-chain blur) — comfortable 60fps headroom; degrade tiers: 256² fields / 40k particles / no refraction layer.

### Learn Mode degradation
Learn Mode is the pool **unfolding, not switching**. On toggle, the camera stays fixed but the water "drains" into four shallow labeled channels over an 800ms eased transition — same shaders, same two-field substance, same colors: (1) the **pitch channel**: the main pool flattened to its vertical axis, semitone isolines now bright and labeled, both epicenters and both filaments plotted against them; (2) the **timing strip**: the ring-phase relationship rendered as two linear wave-trains with onset buoys, crest slippage now measurable in ms; (3) the **vowel basin**: the two cymatic glyphs side by side above a faint F1/F2 chart — the glyph itself *is* the data point, so the user recognizes their own vowel-shapes from the pool; (4) the **timbre well**: the two iridescent color-bodies as swatches with the embedding-distance shown as bleed between them. Numbers appear as quiet captions under living water, never as bars. Tap again and the channels flow back into one pool — the user learns that the pool always *contained* these axes, which retroactively deepens the main experience.

### Risks & mitigations
1. **Identity loss in a shared medium** (two voices dissolving into visual soup mid-attempt). *Mitigation:* two separate simulated fields composited with redundant identity coding — hue pair (teal/gold, CVD-safe), depth layer (refracted vs specular), ring texture — validated by a "squint test" build target: identities must survive 50% gaussian blur.
2. **Diagnostic ambiguity** ("something's off but which dimension?"). *Mitigation:* every dimension owns a non-overlapping visual channel (position=pitch/brightness, phase=timing, moiré=vowel, temperature=timbre), the tether always bows toward the single largest correction, and the replay ghost + one-tap Learn Mode provide slow-brain analysis without polluting the live view.
3. **Visual fatigue / photosensitivity from a perpetually undulating, throbbing field.** *Mitigation:* fixed camera, global amplitude ceiling, idle decays to near-stillness, beat-throb rendered full-field only below 3 Hz (faster beats become localized shimmer between epicenters only), and a user-facing "calm water" intensity setting that scales amplitude/bloom without changing any mapping semantics.
